Introducing Cilindir:

The next evolution in communication technology

Cilindir builds virtual reality (VR) pods. Cilindir pods provide a natural, wearables-free gateway to the virtual world. Each Cilindir is designed to comfortably accommodate one person.

Once inside, an integrated network of cameras continuously monitors the user, powering a realistic 3D avatar. These avatars enable lifelike interactions in shared virtual spaces. When two or more remote users connect through Cilindir, they can see, hear, and interact with each other as if they were together in the same room!

What's more—Cilindir allows users to interact face-to-face with Artificial Intelligence (AI), as if it were a real person. Thanks to full-body capture, AIs can respond naturally to a Cilindir user’s movements and actions.

Technology

360° Immersion

Cilindir offers a fully immersive 3D environment, complete with surround-screen and spatial audio, enabling more natural and expressive interactions.

Full Body Capture

Traditional platforms use cartoonish avatars. Cilindir uses a network of cameras to capture the user’s entire body, capturing lifelike detail and full body language.

No Wearables

Cilindir offers a completely wearables-free experience, eliminating discomfort and fatigue associated with the prolonged use of headsets and hand controllers.

Cross-Platform Integration

Whether you're joining from a Cilindir or a traditional computer setup, you can seamlessly participate in the same virtual meetings/spaces together with other remote users.

Charting the Path to Hyper-Immersion

The Cilindir pods provide a controlled environment, paving the way for advanced features like climate control and holography. These are capabilities that wearables can't provide due to inherent environmental constraints.

Meet the Founder

Michael Luciuk, MSc

Founder and CTO

After moving out of province for graduate school, Michael wanted a more natural and immersive way to stay connected with friends and family back home. Instant messaging and video calls just didn’t offer the immersion and realism needed for family dinners or drinks out with friends.

In 2024, Michael cofounded Cilindir to tackle challenges in remote communication. While researching virtual reality as a platform for corporate communications, Michael realized there was a bigger issue at play: the need to bridge the gap between the physical and digital worlds. This became the foundation for Cilindir’s mission to facilitate human integration into shared virtual spaces, and our platform for networked VR pods.

Michael works as a technical lead at Qoherent, an early-stage tech startup building AI solutions for next-generation wireless technologies. Prior to joining the team at Qoherent, Michael earned his Master’s in Applied Computing (MScAC) from the University of Toronto. Michael’s background is in scientific software development, with professional experience at BASF, Imperial Oil, and the Institute of Space and Atmospheric Studies. He holds bachelor’s degrees in engineering physics and computer science from the University of Saskatchewan.
